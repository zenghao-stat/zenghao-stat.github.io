When do machine learning algorithms work and why?

How do we formalize what it means for an algorithm to learn from data?

How do we use mathematical thinking to design better machine learning methods?

This seminar focuses on developing a theoretical understanding of the statistical properties of learning algorithms.

Schedule

Jul 18, Hao Zeng, Lecture 1

Overview, supervised learning, empirical risk minimization, asymptotic analysis, and concentration inequalities.

Jul 25, Zicheng Xie, Lecture 2

Uniform convergence, finite hypothesis classes, discretizing infinite hypothesis spaces, and Hoeffding inequality.

Cao, Y., Chen, Z., Belkin, M., and Gu, Q. (2022). Benign Overfitting in Two-layer Convolutional Neural Networks. NeurIPS 2022.

Chaudhuri, K., and Hsu, D. (2011). Sample Complexity Bounds for Differentially Private Learning. COLT 2011.

Hopkins, M., Kane, D. M., Lovett, S., and Mahajan, G. (2022). Realizable Learning is All You Need. COLT 2022.

Aug 1, Huajun Xi, Lecture 3

Rademacher complexity, empirical Rademacher complexity, and McDiarmid’s inequality.

Aug 8, Hao Zeng, Lecture 4

Generalization bounds for neural networks, refined generalization bounds, and links to kernel methods.

Aug 15, Zicheng Xie, Lecture 5

Covering numbers, Dudley’s theorem, and their implications.

Aug 29, Huajun Xi, Lecture 6

Generalization bounds for deep nets.
